Betta Fish

With patience and a little persistence, you can teach him to do things like follow your finger, eat from your hand, swim through a hoop, play soccer, and even jump out of the water or come up to be petted. Fish food is the best reinforcement for desired behavior.

  • Scientific Name: Betta splendens
  • Average Length: 2.25 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 3 years
  • Habitat: heavily vegetated marshes, shallow waters of rice paddies, ponds or slow-moving streams in South Asia

A betta fish is a small, freshwater fish that is brightly colored, has long fins and is sometimes called a Siamese fighting fish . Male bettas don't like hanging around other bettas and will fluff up their long, flowy fins and fight, sometimes until one is dead.

Seahorse

These little guys have a big appetite.They mate for life. Men have babies! Their tails are a valuable tool. They have superb camouflage capabilities. Their eyes work independently of one another.

  • Scientific Name: Hippocampus
  • Average Length: 0.6 to 14 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 1 to 5 years
  • Habitat: Temporal and tropical waters throughout the world

Seahorse are unique in appearance, with their horselike head, prehensile tail, independently moving eyes, and brood pouch. They have long, tubular snouts and small, toothless mouths. Their bodies are covered with consecutive rings of bony plates.

Goldfish

Goldfish don't have stomach. They have been known to live for over 40 years. They can recognize peoples faces. They have a memory-span of at least three months.They can grow to over a foot long.

  • Scientific Name: Carassius auratus
  • Average Length: 1 to 2 inches but in the wild often reach 12 to 14 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 15 years
  • Habitat: The wild ancestors of these fish lived in slow-moving waters in China. They thrived even in areas with low oxygen and muddy water.

The common goldfish has two sets of paired fins - the pectoral fins and pelvic fins, and three single fins- the dorsal, caudal, and anal fin. They lack barbels on the upper jaw, and lack scales on the head. They have exceptionally large eyes and acute senses of smell and hearing.
